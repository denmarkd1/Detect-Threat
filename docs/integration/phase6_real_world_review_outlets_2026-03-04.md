# Phase 6 - Real-World Review Outlets and Creator Targets

Date: 2026-03-04

Shortlist of outlets/creators that can evaluate or surface the APK in real-world conditions. Coverage is never guaranteed; treat this as outreach planning.

## 1) Google-authorized security labs (MASA)

Why: strongest formal security signal for Play listing trust.

Requirements (from ADA MASA docs):
- Work directly with an Authorized Lab.
- Pass applicable MASA requirements.
- Recertify yearly.
- AL2 typically costs in the low-thousands USD; AL1 lower-cost path also exists.

Sources:
- https://appdefensealliance.dev/masa
- https://appdefensealliance.dev/masa/faq
- https://appdefensealliance.dev/masa/masa-requirements
- https://appdefensealliance.dev/masa/masa-assessors

## 2) 9to5Google

Why: high-visibility Android news/review outlet.

Requirements/route:
- News tips: `gtips@9to5g.com`.
- Hardware/software review requests: `reviews@9to5mac.com`.
- Tips submission implies permission to publish/syndicate.

Source:
- https://9to5google.com/contact/

## 3) XDA

Why: developer-heavy Android audience.

Requirements/route:
- Use contact channel for Editorial Inquiries (topic ideas, feedback, corrections, suggestions).
- For standards/testing context, XDA publishes review methodology and editorial contacts.

Sources:
- https://www.xda-developers.com/contact
- https://www.xda-developers.com/page/about/

## 4) AndroidB

Why: Android app review and developer submission flow.

Requirements/route:
- Submit through app review form with email, app name, and Play URL.
- App should adhere to Google Play Developer Program Policies and/or be listed on Google Play.
- Submission does not guarantee review.

Source:
- https://www.androidb.com/contact/submit-android-app/

## 5) Tapscape

Why: iOS/Android app review outlet with optional expedited path.

Requirements/route:
- Send review request to `support@tapscape.com`.
- Provide Google Play link and company URL for Android review.
- Optional paid expedited review path is available; merit-based rating still applies.

Source:
- https://www.tapscape.com/submit-app-review/

## 6) Product Hunt (launch feedback channel)

Why: fast real-world feedback from product/early-adopter community.

Requirements/route:
- Launch is free.
- Company accounts are prohibited (use personal maker account).
- Follow launch content checklist and avoid asking directly for upvotes.

Source:
- https://www.producthunt.com/launch

## 7) Techlore (creator)

Why: privacy/security-focused audience aligned with your positioning.

Requirements/route:
- Business inquiries channel supports sponsorships/partnerships/collab.
- They explicitly do not respond to individualized tech-advice requests; outreach must be structured as a review/business pitch.

Source:
- https://techlore.tech/contact/

## 8) Samuel Beckman (creator)

Why: consumer-facing mobile/privacy audience and creator-driven app/tool showcases.

Requirements/route:
- Sponsors and press contact: `business@samuelbeckman.com`.
- Expect press-kit style outreach (what problem is solved, proof, screenshots, test access, and policy-safe claims).

Source:
- https://www.samuelbeckman.com/home
